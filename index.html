Introduction to Candace

Student of California State University, Chico

I am a fourth-year student graduating soon with a degree in Communication Studies (Organizational Communication) and minoring in Public Relations. I am currently searching for entry level positions in California as well as remote opportunities to work once I graduate in May of 2024.

In addition to my studies on campus, I participate in numerous activities including being a club officer, an advising intern, and a dance team captain. These extracurriculars helped me discover that I enjoy working with others to create a sense of community for students. I love to learn all sort of new skills and to be creative in the projects I am passionate about, and I am always looking for new opportunities to learn.

This website started out as an assignment for my JOUR 355: Online Presentation News and PR class, but I use it now to express myself and display my work. I hope you find it easy to navigate and enjoy getting to know me!

Music and Performance

In high school, I learned a K-pop dance for the first time. I told myself, "If I can learn a new dance and perform it, I can get through my APUSH homework."

Music has always been something I really loved. Whether it was band or K-pop, music has played a big role in my life. Being able to perform music that I love has not only become a passion for me, but it has become a big motivator. Seeing the results of my hard work inspire others to learn more has empowered me to continue to share what I love with others.

Last year, a couple members of the LET'S K-POP club on campus were invited to perform at a couple events on campus. We have now transformed into an official school dance team! As co-captains, my friend and I are responsible for organizing many events throughout the year. However, I can confidently say that despite the hardships we've encountered, no member of the team regrets taking part of this experience.

Learning Visual Creator

In my PR class, I had to create a personal profile that could be any format just so my professor can get to know me. Naturally, I stayed up until 5 a.m. finishing the best powerpoint I have ever done.

As a student who is trained to excel in written and oral communication, there is nothing I detest more than a boring text-heavy slide that no one wants to read. Presentations are communication conduits that should be digestable in order for the audience to learn. As I learned more about the different aspects of a presentation, such as design, accessibility, teamwork, and research, I have come to enjoy making and presenting slideshows.

Of course, they are by no means perfect. However, my slides efffectively use visuals to explain concepts to my target audience. In addition, I have picked up new skills about online presentation and branding, including the creation of this portfolio website!

Foodie Favorites

Go-To Dishes When I'm in the Bay Area

  • Cranberry Brie Appetizer
  • Ramen Nagi Ramen
  • Steamed Blackcod  
  • Poke Bowls
  • Apple Pie

Cranberry Brie Appetizer

Dried Cranberries and Brie on Bread Topped With Mint

A delicious and refreshing appetizer was made by my friend Jennifer. It brings back sweet memories of visiting her house and spending time with her after 2 years of Covid-19 isoloation. This was the very first time she made food for me, and she was proud to show off the skills she gained.  

Ramen Nagi Ramen

Chewy ramen noodles with a rich savory soup,topped with meats ad vegetables of choice

Ramen Nagi is a well known ramen shop that makes customized ramen tailored to the customers' taste. My regular order is an original king with firm noodles topped with chashu and cabbage. In addition, my friends and I order chicken karage or potstickers to share.

Steamed Blackcod

Blackcod fish steamed in bean paste with minced garlic and sliced ginger.

One of my favorite dishes to have at home is steamed blackcod. My mother makes a variety of amazing dishes, but I am a huge fan of my mom's cooking skills with fish. The steamed blackcod is flavorful and sauce is great to have with rice. The best part of the fish is the chewy cheek.

Poke Bowls

A bowl of white rice and mixed greens, topped with a poke mix and a selection of add ons.

Poke bowls are another favorite of mine. It can be 100% customized, and it is a quick and filling meal. Whenever I travel from Chico to San Mateo, I make a stop in Sacramento to grab some poke before I continue on my way.

Small Apple Pie

A tiny fist sized apple pie, warm and baked to perfection.

This little treat is from Foreigner Cafe in downtown San Mateo, one of my favorite brunch spots to go to with my friends. I usually grab a coffee and a savory breakfast plate to start, but the warm apple pie really brings the whole meal together.
